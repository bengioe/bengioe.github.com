I'll try to keep this page ordered chronologically; if that's possible, me working on so many things at the same time. ==Pirate Story Online== My first *real* big project was of course, a game. In itself, the game rules and dynamics were never clearly written down, nor were the amateur artists of great assistance. Nevertheless, we were a fun gang, and being the only programmer among it, I wish I'd saved more funny quotes. At it's *apogee*, the game was online with chat, melee combat against monsters(if you can call them that), moving around and having a nice chat with NPCs, and, last but not least, the possibility to have negative HP. There were also some tools like a rudimentary map and sprite editor. The whole thing was written in a mix of Python, C++ with SFML, and alot of comments along the lines of //This shouldn't work but it does. You know, the usual when you're young and budding. You might enjoy watching [http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=10qhG1-BYLA a video] of the early stages of the game. ==PyVariable== I don't like *boost* so much as a C++ tool. It's handy of course, but it can easily become a mess when you don't know how to use it properly. In the spirit of reinventing the wheel, and also discovering more about my favorite language, Python, I decided to make a C++ class which would wrap a PyObject and overload as much operators as decently possible to make the handling of Python objects in C++ easy. It kinda works. You can take a look for yourself on the [http://github.com/bengioe/PyVariable git repo].
